Dear Sir/Ma,
My name is Adesola A. Babaniji, a MUSON-certified music instructor and performer with over a decade of professional experience delivering structured music education to primary and secondary school students across Lagos and Ogun State.
Drawing from extensive teaching experience within reputable institutions, I have developed a refined conservatory model designed to operate within forward-thinking schools that value excellence, discipline, and cultural distinction.

The Conservatory Vision

The DaBOG Young Virtuosos Conservatory is structured as a premium after-school academy built on three core pillars:

  • Structured Curriculum Excellence
  • Measurable Performance Development
  • International Examination Readiness
This program is aligned with MUSON standards and structured to prepare students for internationally recognized graded music examinations such as those administered by the Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music (ABRSM), widely regarded as a global benchmark in music assessment.

Academic & Performance Structure

The conservatory operates in three progressive levels:

  • Beginner
  • Intermediate
  • Advanced
Each level includes:
  • Structured syllabus delivery
  • Technical proficiency benchmarks
  • Performance assessments
  • Stage grooming sessions
  • Examination preparation modules
Instruments offered:
  • Piano
  • Violin
  • Guitar
  • Saxophone
  • Trumpet
  • Flute
  • Clarinet
  • Trombone
  • Recorder
  • Drums
  • Voice & Ensemble Training
  • Music Theory
Music theory may also be integrated into the school’s formal academic structure if desired.

The Signature Recital Experience

To reinforce excellence and institutional prestige, the program culminates in a branded formal showcase:
The Dabog Young Virtuosos Grand Recital This is a professionally coordinated performance event featuring:

  • Formal stage presentation
  • Structured performance order by level
  • Printed recital programs
  • Certificates of Distinction
  • Excellence Awards
  • Professional photography & documentation
This recital serves as both a confidence-building platform for students and a distinguished cultural highlight for the school community.

Proposed Weekly Structure

  • Monday – Piano | Violin
  • Tuesday – Guitar | Recorder
  • Wednesday – Drum | Voice
  • Thursday – Flute | Sax
  • Friday – Trombone | Ensemble
Enrolment per instrument will remain intentionally limited to preserve instructional quality and conservatory-level standards.

Investment & Partnership Structure

As a premium conservatory program, the investment is positioned at:
₦150,000 per pupil per term
This reflects:

  • Structured curriculum delivery
  • Examination readiness preparation (MUSON & ABRSM pathway)
  • Recital production coordination
  • Performance grooming
  • Administrative and operational management
  • Instrument supervision and maintenance
The proposed investment is ₦150,000 per pupil per session, with 5% remitted to the school. I will independently handle all instructional materials, maintenance logistics, and program management to ensure seamless delivery.
I remain open to strategic discussions that align with your institution’s standards, long-term vision and mutual beneficial partnership.
